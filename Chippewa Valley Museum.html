Chippewa Valley Museum
1204 E Half Moon Dr, Eau Claire, WI 54703

The Chippewa Valley Museum is a great place to learn about the history of the
community and to experience the culture of the Chippewa Valley people through
time. It is located in Eau Claire's carson park, which is surrounded by half
moon lake. This beautiful location is also home to the historic logging camp,
the carson park baseball stadium, football fields, tennis courts as well as
picnic pavilions and playgrounds.

The history of the museum begins in 1952 when the Chippewa Valley Historical
society was founded. The museum began as several exhibits in the Eau Claire
public library in 1964 and 1965, then moved to the former Meader Candy
factory in 1966. After several more location changes and a period of having
no museum at all, it was moved to its current location in 1972.
